Our Founding

Our Founding

Founded in 1969, we celebrate our 50th anniversary of Accuracy Accounting. We started out as a small business in Los Angeles and quickly gained recognition from the local community. In 1975, our small city of Walnut invited us to do their taxes and help keep a record of all their expenses. By 1988, Los Angeles recognized us as one of the “Top 100” Businesses in the city. Soon, clients started to become successes and we were very satisfied with the large companies that we were able to help. But in 2012, we refocused our goal on something else: helping small businesses and individuals succeed. We now orient ourselves to become a supporter of unlocked potential instead of worrying about the larger, predominant companies.

What do Accountants Do?

Accountants ensure that financial records are correct by preparing tax returns, making sure financial statements comply with federal and state law, advising management on spending, preparing reports, and meet with private clients to provide financial advice. Accuracy accounting can help with all of the categories in accounting.

What are the types of Accountants?

There are auditors, which keep track of public records and verify financial documents, forensic accountants, which deal with fraud and help the court’s legal issues, tax accountants, which are very trained in taxation and help represent individual taxpayers, financial advisors, which give financial tips to individuals or companies, bookkeepers, which turn accounting transactions into financial statements, and CPAs, experts that cover a variety of different accounting tasks like preparing financial reports and overseeing financial records.

Online outsourced bookkeeping services aid with storing information by using remote servers to keep records online 24/7.

What is a CPA and the difference between it and an accountant?

A CPA, also known as a Certified Public Accountant, is an accountant that passed a rigorous exam and has proven their expertise in the field. They have knowledge in multiple services, such as preparing financial reports, making sure taxes are accurate, and looking over financial records.
